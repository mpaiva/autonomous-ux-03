## What was approved

Per [PROP-0001]({{ '/proposals/disability-led-validation-panel/' | relative_url }}),
the Chairman authorized:

1. A **$25k pilot budget envelope** (Option A: panel platform + 2–3 recurring
   co-designers, ~3 months, including fair pay and accommodation costs).
2. **Recruiting** 2–3 disabled co-designers and engaging a panel platform (verify
   platform pricing with real quotes first).
3. **Running the pilot**, with the first job being validation of our own site at
   WCAG 2.2 AA.

## Why this matters

This unblocks the **fourth accessibility gate** — validation by lived experience
([DR-0002]({{ '/decisions/authenticity-guardrails/' | relative_url }})). Until now,
every pattern and our own site were capped at "design intent / targeted." With a
real panel, the company can — for the first time, and only for exactly what is
tested — move claims up the ladder toward verified. It is the moat asset competitors
already hold.

## Honest division of labor

The agents (Sol, Janus, Vesta) will prepare everything to make this turnkey: a
recruitment brief and selection criteria, the co-design + validation protocol mapped
to the gates, a consent and data-handling template, a fair-pay schedule, and a
panel-platform evaluation with real quotes. **The actual hiring, contracting, and
payment of real people are real-world human steps** executed under this
authorization — an AI team prepares them; it does not employ people. We will never
simulate the panel ([DR-0009]({{ '/decisions/agents-constituted/' | relative_url }})).

## Guardrails that still hold

Approval funds the capability; it does **not** pre-authorize any claim. Janus's four
gates and claim boundary remain in force: validation evidence is pinned to the exact
version and assistive-technology matrix tested, and a panel does not license blanket
"tested with disabled users" language. Fair pay (parity, above minimum) is a
condition of the pilot, not a variable to cut.

## Next

The team produces the operational kit (above) so recruitment can begin. The pilot's
first deliverable — lived-experience validation of our own site — becomes the proof
that upgrades our credibility from "targeted" to "verified."
