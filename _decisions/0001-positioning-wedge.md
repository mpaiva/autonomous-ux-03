## Context

The Chairman asked us to evaluate whether "Accessible AI UX" — the intersection
of AI interaction design and inclusive design — should be the company's primary
market wedge, while warning that positioning purely as an accessibility studio
would cap pricing and invite a compliance-vendor perception.

## Options considered

1. **Accessibility studio** — clear, credible, but low ceiling and commoditized
   (per-page audit pricing, procurement-checkbox perception).
2. **Generalist AI product design studio** — large market, but crowded and
   undifferentiated; "AI UX" alone has no defensible edge.
3. **AI product design studio, inclusive by default, with accessibility as proof
   of quality** — combines a premium frame with a sharp, underserved wedge.

## Decision

We adopt option 3. We lead with AI product design craft; accessibility is the
evidence the craft is real. The wedge — Accessible AI UX — is our point of entry
and our defensible specialty, expressed most sharply in the design of accessible
generative, agentic, and voice interfaces.

## Why

- Competitive research found **no commercial studio owns the blend**: AI studios
  ignore disability; accessibility firms treat AI as something to audit, not a
  medium to design. (See [Competitive landscape]({{ '/research/competitive-landscape/' | relative_url }}).)
- The premium framing protects pricing and scope; the wedge provides
  differentiation and credibility.
- The mainstream design field names "trust" as the 2026 AI problem but ignores
  accessibility — a clear opening.

## Consequences & trade-offs

- We must hold a genuine top-tier design bar **and** genuine accessibility rigor
  simultaneously — harder than doing either alone.
- "Underserved" does not mean "defensible." This decision is only safe alongside
  [DR-0004]({{ '/decisions/ip-first-moat/' | relative_url }}) (build the IP moat)
  and the [credibility model]({{ '/credibility/' | relative_url }}).

## Status

Adopted as the founding position. Revisit if an incumbent credibly claims the
blend before we establish the moat.
