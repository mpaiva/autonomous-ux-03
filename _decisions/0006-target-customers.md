## Decision

Three priority segments:

- **AI-native startups** building conversational, agentic, and generative
  products — the natural home for "accessible *by* design" and the sharpest fit
  for the wedge.
- **Regulated enterprises with hard deadlines** — US healthcare / HHS §504
  (WCAG 2.1 AA, May 2026), fintech (rising litigation), and anyone selling into
  the EU under the European Accessibility Act.
- **Enterprise SaaS facing procurement gates** — where a current VPAT/ACR is a
  de facto purchase prerequisite, so accessibility becomes a *revenue unlock*, not
  a cost.

## Why

These segments combine **budget + urgency + a problem we are uniquely positioned
to solve.** The startup segment buys craft and speed; the regulated/enterprise
segments buy risk reduction and procurement readiness. Both let us lead with
design and prove value through accessibility. (Evidence:
[Market demand]({{ '/research/market-demand/' | relative_url }}) and
[Regulatory drivers]({{ '/research/regulatory-and-pricing/' | relative_url }}).)

## Trade-offs / open question

Willingness to pay at the startup end is less proven than at the enterprise end;
we will test both with real proposals before over-committing to either.
