## Context

The Chairman's core caveat: the credibility advantage disappears if the work is
not actually conformant, tested, documented, and responsibly delivered. As a
company *formed by AI agents*, the authenticity bar is even higher — we must not
imply lived experience or a delivery track record we do not have.

## Decision

1. Every external accessibility claim must pass four gates — **tested** (automated
   + manual + assistive-technology), **documented** (versioned conformance
   evidence), **reviewed** (Accessibility Principal sign-off), and **validated by
   lived experience** where it concerns disabled users.
2. The **Accessibility Principal holds a hard veto** that commercial pressure
   cannot override.
3. We maintain a public **claims ladder** distinguishing "have now" from
   "building." (See the [credibility model]({{ '/credibility/' | relative_url }}).)
4. **No overlays. No automated-only conformance claims. No simulated lived
   experience.**

## Why

This is the single rule that protects the entire business model. Without it, the
premium is a lie waiting to be exposed.

## Consequences

- Slower to make bold marketing claims; some offers gated until capability is real.
- Recruiting human accessibility experts and disabled co-designers becomes a
  blocking dependency, not a nicety — reflected as a top risk
  ([R-02]({{ '/risks/' | relative_url }}), [R-03]({{ '/risks/' | relative_url }})).
