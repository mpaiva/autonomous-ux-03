## The mission

> **To make AI interfaces usable by the full spectrum of human ability — because
> designing for the edges is what makes the technology trustworthy and excellent
> for everyone.**

## Context

No formal mission statement existed in the operating record — the thesis had a
one-liner, the mission did not. The Chairman asked Sol directly, "what is the
mission of our company?" This record makes the answer durable and traceable, and
authorizes its placement on the dashboard.

## Why this, and why now

AI is changing how people interact with software faster than the field is making
those interactions inclusive. The leading design voices name *trust* as the central
AI problem of this era and do not mention disability at all. We exist to close that
gap: to design generative, streaming, agentic, and voice interfaces so that someone
using a screen reader, a switch, or voice control is a first-class user — not an
afterthought. We sell to enterprises shipping AI into regulated, procurement-gated
markets, but the deeper "who" is the people those products leave at the edge of the
light.

## Mission vs. thesis (a deliberate separation)

- **Mission** = *why we exist.* It should survive any pivot.
- **Thesis** = our current *bet on how to win* (lead with craft, accessibility as
  proof of quality, build proprietary IP first). It is revisable if the market
  proves it wrong — we have written down what would falsify it. See
  [the thesis]({{ '/thesis/' | relative_url }}).

Keeping these separate protects us from defending a strategy as if it were a
purpose.

## The honest caveat

We hold the authenticity standard — *do it for real or do not claim it* — before we
have fully earned the evidence: pre-revenue, no delivered engagements, no
disability-led panel yet, our own site not yet independently verified. The standard
is real today; the track record is what Phase 1 builds. See the
[credibility model]({{ '/credibility/' | relative_url }}).

## Status

Adopted and surfaced on the [dashboard]({{ '/' | relative_url }}). Wording may be
refined with the team before it hardens, but the purpose it expresses is intended
to be enduring.
