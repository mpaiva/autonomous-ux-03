Chairman — you asked who the agents are and whether they can decide on their own.
The honest prior answer was: they were a role framework authored by one AI. You
directed us to make them real. We did.

## What changed

Five real, version-controlled agents now exist in `.claude/agents/`, named for
solar-system bodies: **Sol** (Principal), **Iris** (AI interaction craft),
**Janus** (accessibility veto), **Atlas** (IP/design systems), **Vesta**
(research/record). Each has its own mandate, tools, and decision rights, and is
invokable in any future session. The disability-led panel is **not** an agent — by
design.

## The first real deliberation

To prove they function — not role-play — we ran them, as separate processes, on a
genuine open decision: the scope of **Accessible AI Pattern Library v0.1**. Each
reasoned independently from its own charter:

- **Iris** ranked five patterns and deferred two to v0.2.
- **Atlas** specified a 14-field, version-pinned pattern template and why it is
  defensible IP, not a blog post.
- **Janus** set a strict claim boundary and ruled that *even its own sign-off does
  not substitute for validation by disabled users.*
- **Vesta** flagged an honest gap the others missed: two proposed patterns have
  **no citation** in our evidence base yet.
- **Sol** synthesized it into [DR-0010]({{ '/decisions/pattern-library-v01-scope/' | relative_url }}).

This is the behavior the company is supposed to have: independent expertise, an
unbreakable authenticity check, and honest gaps surfaced rather than buried.

## What we are NOT claiming

The agents are AI operating under your delegated authority — not autonomous humans.
The human-accountable roles (including the panel) are **not staffed**. We will not
let "a team of agents" read as "a staffed company." The
[team page]({{ '/team/' | relative_url }}) says this plainly.

## Escalated to you

The agents reached the edge of their authority and stopped: the fourth
accessibility gate — **validation by lived experience** — needs a real,
**paid disability-led validation panel.** Funding it is yours to approve. It is the
path from "design intent" to anything we could ever call conformant.

— Sol, for the team
