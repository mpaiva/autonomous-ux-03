Chairman — here is the first operating update.

## What we did

- **Self-organized** a five-role founding team plus one capability we deliberately
  do *not* yet claim (a disability-led design panel). See
  [Team]({{ '/team/' | relative_url }}).
- **Commissioned cited research** on market demand, the competitive landscape, and
  regulatory/pricing dynamics. See [Research]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}).
- **Made and logged eight founding decisions** with rationale and trade-offs. See
  [Decisions]({{ '/decisions/' | relative_url }}).
- **Built this operating-record site** (Jekyll), designed to target WCAG 2.2 AA —
  proving the standard on ourselves first.

## What we decided

We adopted the thesis: **a premium AI product design studio, inclusive by default,
with accessibility as proof of quality** — not an accessibility studio. All three
strategic options are in play: Accessible AI UX as the wedge, accessibility as the
recurring annuity, and reusable IP as the moat.

## What we learned

The sharpest research finding reframed our priorities: the niche is **underserved
but not automatically defensible.** A taste-plus-services position is absorbable by
a larger AI studio or an accessibility incumbent. So **building proprietary IP and
a disability-led panel is now the top priority**, not a later-stage nicety.

## What is next (Phase 1 — Proof)

1. Ship **Accessible AI Pattern Library v0.1** (first reusable IP).
2. **Verify this site at WCAG 2.2 AA** with assistive-technology testing — and
   publish the result honestly, defects included.
3. Begin recruiting the **human accessibility expertise and disabled co-designers**
   that authenticity requires.

## Risks worth your attention

Top two: **defensibility** (R-01 — mitigated by building IP fast) and
**authenticity** (R-02 — controlled by the accessibility veto and the claims
ladder). Full register: [Risks]({{ '/risks/' | relative_url }}).

## Two items for the Chairman

1. **The "second company."** Your mandate calls this a *second* AI-native design
   company. We have no context on the first — its existence, learnings, or whether
   this company should relate to it. Guidance welcome.
2. **Going public.** This repository is built and committed locally. Publishing it
   publicly would put the company's strategy in the open — an outward-facing,
   hard-to-reverse step. **We are holding it for your explicit approval** rather
   than doing it silently.
