## 1. What it is and why

A standing group of disabled experts who work *with* us as **co-designers**, not just end-stage testers. Co-designers are embedded from the wireframe stage so lived experience shapes a pattern before it hardens — which is cheaper and more honest than auditing a finished build and patching it.

This is the only way to satisfy **gate 4 of our authenticity ladder** — *validated by lived experience* (DR-0002). Janus's sign-off explicitly does **not** substitute for it. Until the panel exists, every pattern stays badged "design intent only" (DR-0010) and **no conformance or lived-experience claim is possible anywhere in the company.** It is the single highest-leverage blocker: it converts our entire IP layer from hypothesis to provable, and it is the moat competitors (Fable's panel, AbilityNet's lived-experience consultants) already hold.

We will never simulate this with an agent (DR-0009). It is real people, paid fairly.

## 2. Composition

Credibility requires breadth, not one "representative" tester. Target coverage across disability type **and** assistive technology:

- **Vision** — blind (screen reader: JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver) and low-vision (magnification, high-contrast, zoom/reflow).
- **Motor** — keyboard-only, switch access, voice control (Dragon, Voice Control).
- **Cognitive / neurodivergent** — attention, memory, dyslexia, autism; relevant to AI surfaces (streaming, agentic flows) where our patterns are weakest (DR-0010 patterns 3 and 5 have no citation today).
- **Hearing** — Deaf/hard-of-hearing; captions, transcripts, voice/text parity.

Aim for intersectionality and a mix of expert AT users and everyday users. Anti-tokenism (§4) starts here: no single person speaks for a disability.

## 3. Operating model

The panel plugs into the **four-gate ladder** and our craft process:

- **Co-design (wireframe stage):** 1–2 panelists review early pattern concepts with Iris; findings logged as evidence.
- **Validation (gate 4):** AT testing on candidate versions; results pinned to the exact version, AT matrix, and date (matching DR-0010's re-verification logic), feeding the conformance report.
- **Cadence:** lightweight monthly co-design touchpoints + validation sprints tied to each release; not a standing payroll burden.
- **Consent/data:** written informed consent, plain-language; participants control recording use; data minimization; right to withdraw without penalty; no PII in published evidence without explicit opt-in.

## 4. Ethics & fair pay

Fair pay for disabled experts is part of our authenticity standard, **not a cost to minimize.**

- **Pay for expertise, not "help."** Benchmark: external usability incentives average ~$64/hr (to ~$81 West Coast); professional participants command ~$118/hr; accessibility consultants ~$42–71/hr. Co-designers are skilled contributors — target the **professional end, $75–150/hr**, with a session minimum so short tasks are not underpaid. *(Confidence: Medium; verify by role.)*
- **Above minimum, with parity.** Disabled experts are paid the same as non-disabled people doing equivalent work — the explicit fair-pay standard, and a deliberate rejection of subminimum-wage norms.
- **Accessible by design:** accessible scheduling, materials, and tools; flexible timing; reasonable accommodations budgeted, not deducted.
- **Anti-tokenism:** multiple voices per disability; co-designers influence decisions (logged), not just react; recurring relationships, not one-off optics.

## 5. Options (tiered — scope vs. cost)

All ranges are **rough, benchmark-derived, not quotes (Low–Medium confidence).**

**(A) Lean pilot — RECOMMENDED.** Existing panel platform (e.g. Fable, pay-as-you-go) for on-demand AT testing + 2–3 recurring co-designers we contract directly for wireframe-stage input.
*Delivers:* gate 4 satisfiable for our own site and v0.1 patterns; real relationships; minimal fixed cost.
*Cost:* roughly **$8k–25k for a 3-month pilot** (platform sessions + co-designer hours). Platform subscription is quote-gated — **verify with Fable directly.**

**(B) Embedded co-design retainer.** A fixed panel of 4–6 paid co-designers on monthly retainer, deeper integration.
*Delivers:* continuous co-design across all IP; strongest credibility.
*Cost:* roughly **$60k–150k/year.** Higher fixed commitment; premature pre-revenue.

**(C) Hybrid — in-house lead + on-demand panel.** Hire/contract a part-time disabled accessibility lead who runs an on-demand platform panel.
*Delivers:* internal accountability + scalable breadth; natural Phase-2 evolution of (A).
*Cost:* roughly **$50k–120k/year** (partial lead comp + per-use platform).

## 6. Phasing & first use

Start with **(A)**. The panel's **first job is validating our own site** against the backlog item *"verify our own site at WCAG 2.2 AA with AT testing."* This dogfoods the standard we sell, produces our **first real lived-experience validation**, and upgrades the credibility ladder from "targeted" to "verified" before we point the panel at client-facing IP. Honest, cheap, and exactly the proof the Chairman's caveat demands.

## 7. Risks

- **Tokenism** — mitigated by multiple voices, decision influence, recurring pay (§4).
- **Recruitment/retention** — disabled experts are in demand; fair pay and respect are the retention strategy. Platform (A) de-risks initial recruitment.
- **Scheduling** — accommodation needs require lead time; build slack into release planning.
- **Over-claiming** — even with a pilot, claims stay scoped to exactly what was validated, version-pinned; Janus's veto holds. A panel does not license blanket "tested with disabled users" language.
- **Cost uncertainty** — ranges are benchmarked, not quoted; treat as planning envelopes pending real quotes.

## 8. The ask

I recommend the Chairman approve:

1. A **pilot budget envelope of $25k** (covers Option A for ~3 months, including platform fees, co-designer pay at professional rates, and accommodation costs).
2. **Authorization to recruit** 2–3 recurring disabled co-designers and to engage a panel platform (verify Fable pricing first).
3. **Authorization to run the pilot**, first job = validate our own site at WCAG 2.2 AA.

**Recommended option: (A), the lean pilot** — it satisfies gate 4 at the lowest fixed cost, builds real relationships, dogfoods our own standard first, and is the natural on-ramp to (C) once revenue justifies it. It is reversible: if it works, we scale; if not, we have lost little and learned much. This unblocks the entire path from "design intent" to anything we can sell as proven.
