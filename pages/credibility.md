This is the load-bearing wall of the whole company. Our premium rests on
accessibility being **authentic**. The moment we claim conformance we have not
tested, or imply capabilities we do not have, the differentiator collapses and we
become a worse-than-average compliance vendor. This page is how we prevent that.

## The claims ladder — what we have vs. what we are building

We separate, explicitly and publicly, the things that are true now from the
things we are working toward. Conflating them would itself be the inauthentic
claim the Chairman warned against.

## How every accessibility claim is governed

Before the company asserts anything about accessibility — in a proposal, a report,
or marketing — it must pass four gates:

1. **Tested** — automated tooling *plus* manual expert review *plus* assistive-
   technology testing with real users. No automated-only claims. No overlays.
2. **Documented** — evidence captured in a conformance report (ACR/VPAT or
   equivalent), tied to the specific version tested.
3. **Reviewed** — signed off by the **Accessibility Principal**, who holds a hard
   veto. Commercial pressure cannot override the veto.
4. **Validated by lived experience** — where we claim something works for disabled
   users, disabled users confirm it. Simulation never substitutes for lived
   experience.

## Standards we hold ourselves to

- **WCAG 2.2 Level AA** as the baseline for our own and clients' interfaces.
- **EN 301 549** for EU-market and procurement contexts.
- **WAI-ARIA Authoring Practices** for interaction patterns.
- **ATAG** principles for any authoring tooling we build.
- For AI interfaces specifically: our own
  [evaluation scorecard]({{ '/ip-roadmap/' | relative_url }}), because no existing
  compliance ruleset covers streaming, agentic, and voice surfaces adequately.

## Our own site is the first test

We refuse to sell a standard we do not meet ourselves. This operating record
**targets WCAG 2.2 AA** and we are verifying it with audit tooling and AT testing.
If you find a barrier here, that is a credibility defect on our part — and we want
it logged as an issue, not excused. Eating our own dog food is not a slogan; it is
the cheapest, most honest proof we can offer.
