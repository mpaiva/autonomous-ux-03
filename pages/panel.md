## 1. Recruitment brief & selection criteria
<p class="text-faint">Owner: Sol</p>

**Role.** Paid **co-designer**, embedded from the wireframe stage through validation
— shaping patterns before they harden, then testing candidate versions on your own
assistive tech. This is skilled, influential work, not free "help": your input
changes design decisions and is logged as named influence. Fair pay per the schedule
(§4): **$100/hr**, 1-hr paid minimum, prep and accommodation time paid.

**Who we're looking for (concrete openings, anchored to the coverage matrix in §2).**
The pilot contracts **two must-have axes directly**: **(1) blind / screen-reader
user** (JAWS, NVDA, or VoiceOver) and **(2) cognitive / neurodivergent** (attention,
memory, dyslexia, autism). An **optional third** deepens either axis or adds
intersectionality. These two axes are non-substitutable for agentic and streaming AI
surfaces — exactly our weakest patterns. Low-vision, motor, and Deaf/HoH coverage is
**filled on-demand via the platform now** and brought into the standing panel as we
scale; we are seeding a panel, not staffing all of it.

**What we value.** Lived expertise using AT with AI, agentic, and streaming
interfaces; the ability to **articulate and influence** design decisions — to say
why something fails and what would work — not just react to finished screens. A mix
of expert and everyday AT use is welcome.

**Engagement model.** Remote; lightweight **monthly** co-design touchpoints plus
validation sprints tied to releases — roughly **a few hours a month** in the pilot,
not a full schedule. Accessible by design: accessible scheduling, materials, and
tools; flexible timing; accommodations budgeted (never deducted). Pay band
$75–150/hr; we pay **$100/hr** (§4).

**Selection process (fair, accessible).** Express interest in a short message or a
quick conversation in whatever format suits you — **no unpaid "audition" or sample
task**. Accommodations are offered from first contact. We decide on relevant lived AT
expertise and the ability to influence design, aiming for one person per must-have
axis; any paid trial session is **paid at the standard rate**. Consent is walked
through before anyone signs (§5).

**Sourcing routes.** Disability-led organizations and communities; **Knowbility
AccessWorks** for direct recruiting (request a recruiting-only quote — see §6); the
panel platform's network (**Fable**) for on-demand breadth; and trusted referrals
from disabled professionals. Reach people where they are, in accessible formats.

## 2. Panel coverage & selection criteria (credibility breadth)
<p class="text-faint">Owner: Janus</p>

Credibility requires breadth across disability type **and** the AT actually used —
not one "representative" tester.

<div class="table-wrap">

| Axis | AT to cover | Pilot status |
|---|---|---|
| Vision — blind | Screen reader: JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver | **Must-have (human)** |
| Cognitive / neurodivergent | Attention, memory, dyslexia, autism profiles | **Must-have (human)** |
| Vision — low-vision | Magnification, high-contrast, zoom/reflow | Fill via platform / scale |
| Motor | Keyboard-only, switch access, voice (Dragon, Voice Control) | Fill via platform / scale |
| Deaf / hard-of-hearing | Captions, transcripts, voice/text parity | Fill via platform / scale |

</div>

**Why blind+SR and cognitive are the must-have human axes.** Our weakest patterns are
agentic (DR-0010 #3) and AI-disclosure (#5) — both zero-citation today.
Agentic/streaming surfaces stress exactly two things: **cognitive load** (status,
oversight, interruption, pacing) and **screen-reader output** (live regions, focus,
per-message structure). With only 2–3 co-designers, that is where lived experience is
non-substitutable; the rest is filled on-demand via the platform and broadened as we
scale.

**Anti-tokenism rules:** multiple voices per disability (no single person speaks for
a disability type); a mix of expert and everyday AT users, seeking intersectionality;
co-designers **influence decisions, logged** as named influence (not collected as
reaction after the fact); recurring relationships, not one-off optics.

## 3. Co-design & validation protocol (mapped to the four gates)
<p class="text-faint">Owner: Janus</p>

A panelist does **two distinct things**. Neither is "a gate"; together they feed
gates 1–4.

1. **Co-design (wireframe stage, with Iris).** 1–2 panelists review an early pattern
   concept before it hardens. Their input shapes the design and is **logged as
   evidence** with named influence. This is craft input, not a conformance claim.
2. **Build & internal test (gates 1–3).** The candidate version is tested: automated
   tooling **plus** manual expert review **plus** AT testing; documented in a
   versioned report; Janus signs off (gate 3).
3. **Validation (gate 4 — lived experience).** Panelists run AT testing on the
   **exact candidate version**. Results are pinned and feed the conformance report.
   This is the lived-experience gate; nothing else satisfies it.

**Pinning (consistent with DR-0010 re-verification logic).** Every validation result
is pinned to a triple: **version tested + assistive-technology matrix + date**. The
pin is the unit of validity. When any of the three changes, the claim lapses and
re-verification is required.

**Claim rule (binding):**
- A completed validation lets us say **only**: "this surface/pattern, at this
  version, was validated by disabled users on this AT matrix, on this date."
  Triple-scoped. Nothing broader.
- It **never** licenses blanket "tested with disabled users," "accessible," or
  "conformant."
- **Janus's sign-off (gate 3) does NOT substitute for gate 4.** Review by the
  Accessibility Principal is necessary, never sufficient; lived-experience validation
  cannot be replaced by approval or simulation.

**Per-session validation checklist** (each session must record all, or the evidence
is inadmissible): version tested (build/commit/spec ID); AT matrix (AT + version + OS
+ browser per tester); date; scope tested; what was **not** tested (so the claim
can't drift); findings with severity + WCAG mapping (mapping, not a pass-claim);
panelist(s) and consent recorded.

## 4. Fair-pay schedule
<p class="text-faint">Owner: Vesta · figures are planning estimates, not quotes (verify by role)</p>

**Principle (a condition of the pilot, not a variable to cut — DR-0014):** disabled
co-designers are paid **at parity with non-disabled experts** doing equivalent work,
**above minimum wage, never subminimum.** Pay is for expertise, not "help."

<div class="table-wrap">

| Item | Rate / term |
|---|---|
| Co-designer hourly band | **$100/hr** (within the approved $75–150 band; professional end) |
| Session minimum | **1 hr paid minimum** — short tasks are never underpaid |
| Prep / review / accommodation **time** | Paid at the **same $100/hr** — never deducted |
| Prompt payment | Net-**15** from invoice/session; faster on request |

</div>

**Accommodation _costs_** (distinct from paid time): captioning, sign-language
interpreters, assistive tech, and accessible-materials production — **budgeted as a
line, not deducted from pay.**

## 5. Consent & data-handling template (plain language)
<p class="text-faint">Owner: Vesta · offered in large print, plain-text, screen-reader-friendly, and audio on request — and walked through verbally before signing</p>

**What this is.** You're joining Penumbra as a paid co-designer. This explains what
we record, how we use it, and your rights. Ask us anything before you sign.

**What we record.** Notes on what works and what doesn't, your task steps, and the
assistive technology you use. We record **audio/video only with your separate opt-in
below.**

**How we use it.** To fix and improve designs, and as internal evidence pinned to the
exact version and AT tested. We collect **only what we need** — nothing more.

**No personal details in published work.** We never put your name, face, voice, or
identifying details in any published or client-facing material **unless you opt in,
in writing, for that specific use.** By default you are anonymous.

**Recording & clip reuse (opt-in — tick only what you allow):**
- ☐ You may record this session for internal notes.
- ☐ You may reuse short clips **internally** (team only).
- ☐ You may reuse short clips **externally / publicly** (named or anonymous — your choice).

**Storage & retention.** Stored securely, access limited to the project team. Kept
for the pilot plus **12 months**, then deleted. You can ask for your data sooner.

**Your right to withdraw.** You can stop, skip a task, or leave at **any time, for any
reason, with no penalty — you are still paid for time given.** You can withdraw
consent for recordings later, and we delete them.

Signature block confirms fair pay, parity, and that accommodations are provided at no
cost to the co-designer.

**Recommendation for the lean pilot.** Use **Fable Engage pay-per-project (~$3,750,
vendor-published)** for on-demand AT testing — lowest fixed cost, no subscription
lock-in, fits the envelope. Recruit the 2–3 recurring co-designers **directly**
(AccessWorks is the strongest sourcing route — request a recruiting-only quote).
**Skip Applause** — enterprise scale is overkill pre-revenue. Hold **Accessible.org's
$750/session** as a transparent backup. **Get a real Fable and AccessWorks quote
before committing.**

*Confidence: Fable / Accessible.org per-unit prices High (vendor-published); annual
aggregate figures Low (third-party estimates); AccessWorks / Applause / Fable-annual
quote-gated.*
